Nine Menâ€™s Morris is an ancient mill game, dating at least from Roman times. It is the most prominent of all the mill games, played all around the world, but particularly in central European countries. #Play

The game (as most mill games) is split into two phases. During the first (placement) phase, the players take turns placing a single piece at a time onto one of the vacant points on the board. Once all the pieces have been placed, the movement phase begins. In this part of the game, players take turns moving a single piece along a line to another vacant point. Once a player is reduced to three pieces, their pieces can â€˜flyâ€™ and move to any empty point on the board.

Throughout the game, each time a player forms a mill they remove any piece of their opponentâ€™s that is not part of a mill. If all their opponentâ€™s pieces are in mills, no piece may be removed.

During the movement phase, it is possible to form two mills simultaneously. In this case the player may remove two of the opponentâ€™s pieces from the board.

A player loses the game when they are reduced to fewer than three pieces, or if they are unable to make a valid move on their turn.

When played on a board with diagonals, mills are not usually permitted to be made on the diagonal lines. However, this varies according to location and time.
