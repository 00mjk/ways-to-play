Oicho-Kabu
Draft

Oicho-Kabu (おいちょかぶ, ‘eight-nine’) is a Japanese gambling game in the style of Baccarat.

The game is played with special cards called kabufuda (株札, ‘kabu cards’). It can also be played with hanafuda cards (by dropping two months),There are also hanafuda decks with only 10 months, called mushifuda. These lack the 6th & 7th months of the standard deck. or with the A–9 from a standard deck of playing cards.

The game originated in Japan, but has surprisingly also been spotted amongst Aboriginal communities in the northern parts of Australia, apparently having been transferred there by Japanese sailors working in the pearl industry.ab A Nintendō advertising poster from the early 20th century also indicates that their cards were being exported to Australia. In Australia the game is played with standard “international” playing cards.

#Play

Up to ten people can play the game (aside from the dealer).

#Terminology

The hand totals are counted with special gambling-specific numbers. Some of these — like the playing cards themselves — are derived from Portuguese:

Total Standard Gambling Meaning
0 〇 rei buta ‘pig’
1 一 ichi pin From Portuguese pinta, ‘spot’
2 二 ni nizou
3 三 san santa/sanzun
4 四 shi/yon yotsuya ‘set of four arrows’
5 五 go goke/gosu
6 六 roku roppou
7 七 nana/shichi naki/shichiken
8 八 hachi oicho From Portuguese oito, ‘eight’
9 九 kyū kabu Possibly from an Edo-period word for nine, kau

#Variants

#Kabu

This is a simplified variant by Sid Sackson, for 2–6 players, described in Card Games Around the World (p. 12).

Play with the A–⑩ from a standard deck of cards. You will also need some chips to keep track of score. Sids’ suggested chip distribution for each player is:

Players 1 pt
(White)		 5 pt
(Red)		 10 pt
(Blue)		 Total Points
2 10 2 2 40
3 10 3 3 55
4 10 4 4 70
5 10 5 5 85
6 10 6 6 100

Shuffle and deal two cards face-down to each player. Each player looks at their cards and may then request a third, and subsequently a fourth card.

The goal, as in standard Oicho-Kabu, is to get a total as close to 9 as possible, discarding ⑩s.

After all players have finished requesting new cards, the hands are revealed. Each player collects the difference from their hand to players with a lower count, and pays the different from their hand to players with a higher count.

The game ends when a player is unable to pay their debts as they have run out of chips. They do not pay any of their remaining chips. Whoever has the most points is the winner.

Variant: in addition to dealing to the players, deal 6 cards face-up to the table. These cards are out of play but give the players more information about the cards remaining in the deck.
