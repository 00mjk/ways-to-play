Horseshoe
Draft

This is a simple children’s game that seems to originate from China, where it is known by many names. Because the board is shaped like the Chinese character ‘区’ (‘area’), it is known as ‘area character game’ 区字棋. Other names include ‘crotch game’ 裤裆 棋, or ‘scissor game’ 剪刀棋. (TODO: transliterations!)

In Cantonese it is called 崩口棋 bong1 hau2 kei4,a[p. 100]bIn older books (such as Culin’s) this is transliterated as “pong hau k’i”. which means ‘cleft lip game’ (based on the shape of the board).

In Korea it is known as 우물고누 umulgonu ‘well game’ or 강고누 ganggonu ‘river game’.a[p. 100] In Punjabi it is called dō-guṭi (TODO: possibly ਦੋਗੁਟਿ?).c[p. 143]

In Thailand it is called “sua tok tong” (เสือ… suea… ‘tiger…’?).

Refs: [The Oxford History of Board Games; Math Games & Activities from Around the World, p. 32]
