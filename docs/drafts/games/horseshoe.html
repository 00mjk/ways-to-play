    Horseshoe

    This is a simple children’s game that seems to originate from China, where it is known by many names. Because the board is shaped like the Chinese character ‘区’ (‘area’), it is known as ‘area character game’ 区字棋. Other names include ‘crotch game’ 裤裆棋, or ‘scissor game’ 剪刀棋. (TODO: transliterations!)

    * In older books (such as Culin’s) this is transliterated as “pong hau k’i”.

    In Cantonese it is called 䦁口棋bong1 hau2 kei4,* which means ‘mattock blade chess’. An equivalent Mandarin term is 鋤chú棋qi ‘hoe chess’.

    In Korea it is known as 우물고누umulgonu ‘well game’ or 강고누ganggonu ‘river game’.‌1 (100) In Punjabi it is called dō-guṭi (TODO: possibly ਦੋਗੁਟਿ?).‌2 (143)
